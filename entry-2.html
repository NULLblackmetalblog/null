Entry II — LUNAR AMULET - Yoke of Oblivion

LUNAR AMULET’s self-titled EP, released in December 2025, stands as a stark testament to the atmospheric depths possible within black metal. Hailing from Portland, Maine, Lunar Amulet is the solo project of Aaron Charles — known for his work in acts such as Falls of Rauros, Rhûn and Blood Chariot — and this release sees him forging an austere, elemental sound steeped in naturalism, mythic atmosphere and bleak urgency. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Yoke of Oblivion exemplifies this approach: distant guitars and patient riffing unfold with ritualistic weight, giving space to cold air and heavy silence rather than polish or aggression. The track evokes a burden older than the soil and a stillness that outlives those who bear it — an apt metaphor for the unforgiving terrain that black metal’s rawest strains inhabit. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

This entry documents one such movement within the raw black metal continuum — uncompromising, atmospheric, and unafraid of isolation. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
