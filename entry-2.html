Entry II — Yoke of Oblivion (Lunar Amulet)

“Yoke of Oblivion” embodies the core of raw black metal: distant production, suffocating atmosphere, and repetition as ritual rather than structure.

The recording rejects polish and clarity, replacing them with weight, isolation, and a sense of erosion that unfolds slowly and without relief.

This is not music for consumption. It is a document of atmosphere and discipline, indifferent to the listener’s comfort or expectation.
